Student Figure Design & Visualization Group

Design Den

A biweekly, student-led group focused on improving scientific figure design, data visualization, and research communication skills.

Make your science look as good as it is.

Design Den creates a low-pressure space for students and researchers to build practical design skills that are essential for communicating science clearly and effectively, but are not always formally taught in graduate training.

Sessions alternate between short, hands-on tutorials and informal co-working or critique sessions. Tutorial topics may include Adobe Illustrator basics, figure layout, visual hierarchy, color choices, polishing R/ggplot or Python figures, poster design, and preparing visuals for presentations or publications.

Participants are encouraged to bring their own R plots, Python figures, posters, slides, or works in progress for feedback and publication-ready polish.

Learn

Short tutorials on Adobe Illustrator, figure layout, visual hierarchy, and polishing scientific graphics.

Bring

R plots, Python figures, posters, slides, manuscripts, or any visual work in progress.

Get Feedback

Casual, constructive critique focused on clarity, accessibility, and publication-ready figure design.
